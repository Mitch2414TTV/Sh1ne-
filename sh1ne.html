Write Protection

Write Protect is an annoying thing. It stops you from editing the firmware of your Chromebooks. This can be removed; to disable write protection, you would have to do one of many things. In some models of Chromebooks like the 3100, you would just have to unplug the battery and then plug it in with a charger; this would disable write protection temporarily. In some other cases, such as older models like the Chromebook Dell 11 3180, you have to unscrew a WP screw; this will disable write protection as long as the screw is out. While in some other cases, it's not that simple; you would have to use a method called tsunami. This method involves shorting two pins, and it is very dangerous. I would not recommend it unless you know what you're doing.

If you want more information on this just search it videos on how to remove write protection are everywhere even for specific models.
